MODELING abstract neurons

Detailed Description

Information and computing play a fundamental role in understanding neuronal operation. The goal was set decades ago: 'The ultimate aim of computational neuroscience is to explain how electrical and chemical signals are used in the brain to represent and process information'. [31] 'The brain computes! This is accepted as a truism by the majority of neuroscientists.' [18] Despite the impressive advances in neuroscience during the passed decays, there are still 'white spots'. For example, 'Why action potentials are initiated in the axon is still unclear' [19] and "we should not seek a special organ for `information storage'--it is stored, as it should be, in every circuit" [35]. This latter source points to the important point 'Send only information that is needed, and send it as slowly as possible' [35], because 'Communication consumes 35 times more energy than computation'. [20] One more point why computation and communication must not be handled separately.

Nature uses an infinite variety of implementing neurons. However, they can cooperate with each other, proving that they implement some general principles. As [17] forms the idea in their Introduction, 'The fundamental task of the nervous system is to communicate and process information'. ... 'The basic structural units of the nervous system are individual neurons, and neurons convey neural information by virtue of electrical and chemical signals. ... Despite the extraordinary diversity and complexity of neuronal morphology and synaptic connectivity, the nervous systems adopts a number of basic principles for all neurons and synapses.'

Correspondingly, we base our discussion on those general basic principles and create an "abstract neuron", skipping the actual implementation details nature applies to the cited references. We follow the line [17] started: we introduce the in the meantime discovered finer details and new results of physiological research. We focus on introducing the role of the time [37] into that abstract operation where the "slow" ionic currents play a major role and revisit the range of applicability of approximations and abstractions furthermore the adequate intrpretation of physical laws and mechanisms underlying them. Those hints show the way towards interpreting neuronal information [38] [39] and enable establishing neuronal computing on the generalized computing theory [40]

When studying CNS, we need to start from cells to circuits [29], and we can study the brain "from inside out" [6]. During this journey we need to keep in mind that we need to use (and with the growing complexity: change) the abstractions and approximations used to describe the abstract operation. Sometimes even in describing the same phenomenon different approximations must be used for the different participating interactions.
