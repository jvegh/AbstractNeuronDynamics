§ 2.3.1 Stokes’ Law

The most known and influencing axon current measurement has been published in 1952 [3]. They used a single-axon input and measured the neuronal membrane’s current, which in this way was identical to the axon current. The diffused-in ions are transported towards the membrane as a “slow” macroscopic ionic current (the speed of current HH  [3] measured and also theoretically derived to be about 20⁢m/s; it is in the order of magnitude we mentioned for the speed of macroscopic currents in metals and electrolytes.

They measured the time course of the axonal current at different clamping voltages. Their result is reproduced in Fig. 2.4. As we discussed, ions diffuse in the axon’s wall, producing a saturation-type current. Our simple model assumes that α is constant in time but (through v and d⁢Vd⁢x) depends on the clamping voltage).
